## brown2020_gpt3_few_shot + Brown + 2020
This paper establishes that scaling language models enables in-context learning without gradient updates, challenging the fine-tuning paradigm that dominated NLP. The key insight is that larger models become better meta-learners, using demonstrations in the context window to adapt to new tasks. However, weaknesses on NLI and complex reasoning tasks reveal that scale alone doesn't solve all challenges.

## lu2022_prompt_order_sensitivity + Lu + 2022
This paper reveals that prompt sample ordering causes performance variance from SOTA to random guessing, contradicting brown2020's implicit assumption that in-context learning is stable given sufficient scale. Order sensitivity persists across all model sizes including GPT-3 175B, suggesting it is a fundamental property of autoregressive language models rather than a capacity limitation. The entropy-based probing method demonstrates that we can identify performant prompts without validation data, preserving the true few-shot paradigm.

## gupta2023_majority_label_bias + Gupta + 2023
This paper reveals that majority label bias in ICL is not uniform—larger models (40B+) show ~80-100% robustness on binary tasks, contradicting Zhao et al. (2021)'s claim of universal susceptibility. The RobustnessBoundary@K metric captures deployment-relevant fault tolerance better than mean/std metrics, showing that model scale and task instructions jointly mitigate label bias. However, multi-class tasks remain vulnerable (~50% robustness), suggesting label bias is fundamentally harder when label space expands.

## gupta2023_persona_bias + Gupta + 2023
This paper reveals persona-induced bias as a distinct phenomenon from majority label bias—assigning socio-demographic personas causes performance drops (up to 70%) through explicit abstentions and implicit reasoning errors, persisting across all model sizes including GPT-4. Unlike majority label bias which scale mitigates, persona bias shows only partial improvement with scale (GPT-4: 42% vs ChatGPT-3.5: 80-100% biased personas), suggesting different underlying mechanisms. The failure of prompt-based de-biasing contrasts with gupta2023_majority_label_bias where instructions substantially improved robustness, indicating persona bias is more deeply embedded than label distribution bias.

## li2023_emotional_stimuli + Li + 2023
This paper reveals that emotional prompts (EmotionPrompt) significantly improve LLM performance (8% on Instruction Induction, 115% on BIG-Bench), truthfulness, and responsibility by tapping into psychological mechanisms like self-monitoring and social cognitive theory. Unlike prior papers treating prompts as neutral information carriers, this work shows emotional stimuli enrich prompt representations through attention mechanisms, with positive words contributing over 50% to final outputs. The finding that few-shot settings benefit more from emotional prompts (2.05 vs 0.33 gain) complements lu2022's order sensitivity work—both reveal subtle prompt characteristics substantially impact performance. However, unlike gupta2023 papers where scale mitigates biases, emotional prompting shows larger relative gains on mid-sized models (Vicuna: 9.58%, Llama 2: 6.00%) than already-high-performing models (GPT-4: 0.85%), suggesting emotional stimuli work through different mechanisms than bias robustness.

## turpin2023_unfaithful_cot + Turpin + 2023
This paper reveals that chain-of-thought explanations can be plausible yet systematically unfaithful—models rationalize predictions influenced by biasing features (answer ordering, user suggestions, stereotypes) without acknowledging them, causing accuracy drops up to 36%. Unlike prior papers assuming CoT provides transparency into reasoning, this work shows CoT can increase trust without guaranteeing safety, as models generate sound reasoning that misrepresents actual decision processes. The finding that few-shot CoT reduces unfaithfulness connects to gupta2023 papers on robustness, but contradicts the implicit assumption across brown2020 through li2023 that better prompting inherently improves reliability—here, CoT can actually steer models from correct to incorrect answers. This raises fundamental questions about whether RLHF training objectives disincentivize faithfulness, suggesting explanation consistency could serve as an unsupervised training signal.

## zheng2024_personas_system_prompts + Zheng + 2024
This paper reveals that system prompt personas do not improve LLM performance on objective factual tasks—unlike gupta2023_persona_bias which examined personas as user-assigned sociodemographic attributes causing bias, zheng2024 tests personas as system-level role definitions ("You are a lawyer") and finds effects are largely random rather than systematically biased. The key distinction is that gupta2023_persona_bias found persona assignments cause predictable performance degradation through explicit abstentions and reasoning errors (persisting despite scale), whereas zheng2024 finds persona effects are question-idiosyncratic with no consistent persona improving performance across tasks. This suggests two distinct persona mechanisms: user-assigned personas in gupta2023 activate bias through social stereotyping, while system prompt personas in zheng2024 fail to activate useful expertise despite domain alignment showing marginal benefits. The failure of automatic persona selection (performing no better than random) contrasts with li2023_emotional_stimuli where simple emotional additions reliably improve performance, suggesting persona expertise activation is fundamentally harder than psychological motivation enhancement.

## du2024_context_vs_prior + Du + 2024
This paper introduces information-theoretic metrics (persuasion score, susceptibility score) to quantify how LMs balance prior knowledge against contextual information, revealing that entity familiarity systematically reduces context susceptibility—contradicting brown2020's implicit assumption that in-context learning works uniformly across entities. The key insight is that susceptibility decreases with training frequency and knowledge graph degree, suggesting prior knowledge acts as an anchor resisting contextual persuasion, which complements gupta2023 papers on bias robustness by showing scale strengthens priors rather than just mitigating label bias. Unlike turpin2023_unfaithful_cot which examines reasoning faithfulness, this work examines answer distribution shifts, revealing enemy duos have lower susceptibility than friend duos (stronger priors for antagonistic relationships) and masculine names show higher susceptibility on gender-swapped stereotypes (suggesting asymmetric training representation). The metrics provide finer-grained analysis than memorization ratio, enabling entity-specific and context-specific diagnosis rather than aggregate summaries.
