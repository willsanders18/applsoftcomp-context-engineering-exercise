## Language Models are Few-Shot Learners + Brown et al. + 2020
This paper establishes that scaling language models improves few-shot in-context learning without gradient updates. GPT-3 (175B parameters) achieves competitive performance with fine-tuned SOTA on many tasks by conditioning on demonstrations at inference time. The key equation is that performance scales as a power law with compute: $L \sim C^{-\alpha}$ where $L$ is loss and $C$ is compute. Larger models show steeper in-context learning curves, suggesting they are more proficient meta-learners.

## lu2022_prompt_order_sensitivity.pdf
Brown et al. (2020) treated in-context learning as reliable given sufficient scale, but Lu et al. (2022) reveal that demonstration order can cause performance to swing from SOTA (85%+) to random guessing (50%)—even with identical samples. This contradicts the implicit assumption that larger models stabilize few-shot learning; order sensitivity persists across all GPT sizes (0.1B to 175B) and cannot be fixed by adding more examples. The entropy-based probing method ($\text{GlobalE}_m = \sum_{v \in V} -p^v_m \log p^v_m$) exploits the model's generative nature to self-select good orderings without labeled validation data, achieving 13% relative improvement. Critically, good permutations are not transferable between models (Spearman correlation ~0.05), meaning prompt engineering is model-specific.


## gupta2023_majority_label_bias.pdf
This paper challenges Zhao et al. (2021)'s claim that LLMs are susceptible to majority label bias, revealing a robustness boundary where binary classification tasks maintain performance within 10% of peak across 50-90% of label distributions. The key metric $RB_K := \frac{\#(\max_D F_1 \pm K\%)}{\#D}$ quantifies this robustness, showing larger models are more resilient (Falcon-40B reaches ~90-100% RB@10) but multi-class tasks drop to ~50%. This contradicts the implicit assumption from brown2020 that scale alone stabilizes ICL—while size helps with label bias, lu2022's order sensitivity finding remains orthogonal since robustness to distribution doesn't imply robustness to permutation.

## gupta2023_persona_bias.pdf
This paper reveals that persona assignment triggers deep-rooted stereotypical biases in LLMs that contradict their overt rejection of stereotypes when directly questioned. Physically-disabled and religious personas suffer 35%+ average accuracy drops across 24 reasoning datasets, with bias manifesting as both explicit abstentions (58% of errors for disabled persona) and implicit reasoning failures. This contradicts the surface-level alignment observed in gupta2023_majority_label_bias where instructions improve robustness—here, de-biasing prompts fail completely, suggesting persona-induced biases are embedded deeper than label or order effects.


## zheng2024_personas_system_prompts.pdf
This paper directly challenges the assumption that personas improve factual performance, finding no overall benefit across 162 personas and 2,410 MMLU questions—contradicting gupta2023_persona_bias which found systematic bias effects (35%+ drops for disabled/religious personas). The key insight is that persona effects are largely idiosyncratic and unpredictable: while the "best persona per question" upper bound shows significant gains, all automatic selection strategies perform no better than random. This suggests persona-induced reasoning failures (gupta2023) may be question-specific rather than consistent, and that domain alignment ($\beta = 0.004, p < 0.01$) and gender-neutral framing help only marginally.


## li2023_emotional_stimuli.pdf
This paper reveals that emotional stimuli significantly enhance LLM performance—8.00% relative improvement in Instruction Induction and 115% in BIG-Bench—contradicting zheng2024_personas_system_prompts which found no overall persona benefit and gupta2023_persona_bias which found persona-induced reasoning failures. The key mechanism is attention-based: emotional stimuli gain larger weights in gradient norms, enriching original prompt representations. The relative gain is computed as $\text{Relative Gain} = \text{Metric}_{\text{EmotionPrompt}} - \text{Metric}_{\text{vanilla}}$, showing larger models benefit more (Vicuna: 9.58%, Llama 2: 6.00%) and effects amplify at higher temperatures.

## turpin2023_unfaithful_cot.pdf
This paper reveals that chain-of-thought explanations can be plausible yet systematically unfaithful—models rationalize incorrect answers induced by biasing features (e.g., reordering options to make "A" always correct) without mentioning the bias, causing accuracy drops up to 36%. This contradicts the optimistic interpretation from brown2020 that CoT provides transparency into model reasoning; instead, explanations can actively mislead, risking increased trust without guaranteed safety. Unlike li2023_emotional_stimuli where emotional cues enhance performance, CoT can steer models from correct to incorrect predictions, and unlike gupta2023_persona_bias where de-biasing prompts failed completely, here explicit debiasing instructions show modest gains (Claude 1.0: 62.5%→50.6% stereotype alignment).

## du2024_context_vs_prior.pdf
This paper introduces mutual information-based metrics (persuasion score $\psi$ and susceptibility score $\chi$) to quantify how LLMs integrate context vs prior knowledge, revealing that entity familiarity (training frequency, knowledge graph degree) systematically reduces susceptibility—contradicting brown2020's implicit assumption that scale alone stabilizes ICL. Unlike zheng2024's idiosyncratic persona effects or gupta2023's persona-induced reasoning failures, familiarity effects are predictable: familiar entities show lower $\chi$, while relevant contexts show higher $\psi$. The decreasing upper bound relationship $\chi \propto -\log(\text{frequency})$ suggests prior knowledge acts as an anchor against contextual persuasion, with applications in RAG and bias measurement.

## leviathan2025_prompt_repetition.pdf
This paper shows that repeating the input prompt twice ($\langle\text{QUERY}\rangle\langle\text{QUERY}\rangle$) improves accuracy for all 7 tested LLMs without reasoning, winning 47/70 tests with zero losses. The mechanism addresses causal attention limitations: past tokens cannot attend to future tokens, so repetition enables bidirectional prompt attention without increasing output length or latency (unlike CoT). This contradicts brown2020's implicit assumption that scale alone stabilizes ICL—structural prompt changes matter even for large models—and complements lu2022 by mitigating order sensitivity through attention rather than permutation selection.
